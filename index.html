Rates of Detection: Bobcat, Coyote and Grey Fox

2014-2017

This application displays rates of detection by month for the for north array of cameras. A rate of detection is the number of species seen during each event, normalized by 100 working trap nights. The number of species per event is the maximum number of species detected in a burst of camera images. The burst is taken from the first image trigger, until there is a quiet time of five seconds between one image and the next.

How to use the app:

  • 1. Graduated symbols represent rates of detection by species as referenced in the legend where 100 is the highest recorded value and 50 is the median.
  • 2. Select species of interest from dropdown menu (all or indivdual).
  • 3. Use time slider on lower left of screen to cycle through months and years of interest.
  • 4. When time period of interest is determined hover mouse over camera location of interest to get a normalized rate of detection by species.
